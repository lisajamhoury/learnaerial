Buying Basic Aerial Equipment

Aerial equipment ranges from the relatively straightforward to the incredibly complicated. Here are a few places where you can begin to build your knowledge and rigging arsenal.

REMEMBER! Rigging without proper knowledge can be life threatening. Talk to an experienced aerialist and/or professional rigger before buying or attempting to rig your own equipment.

  • Aerial Essentials on YouTube

    www.youtube.com/user/AerialEssentials
    Aerial Essentials has helpful basic videos for learning to rig aerial silks and hammock.

  • Amazon

    www.amazon.com
    Most basic rigging equipment can be found on Amazon. These are the basics that I use for aerial silks and hammock rigging: Omega Pacific Steel Screw-Lok Carabiner, Big 8 Stainless Steel Rescue Descender, Petzl Large Pro Swivel, Easton Pro Rock Rosin.

  • Barry Aerial Equipment

    www.barry.ca
    Barry Aerial Equipment sells a variety of aerial apparatuses and rigging.

  • Custom Built Equipment

    www.cbe-circus.com
    CBE sells several different types of aerial apparatuses (including rope, lyra, silks, straps, trapeze) and rigging.

  • Fabric Depot

    www.fabricdepotco.com
    Many aerialists buy their aerial fabric (aerial silks) here. See page 31 of the current catalog for the specific fabric.

  • Mutual Hardware

    www.mutualhardware.com
    Mutual Hardware is located in Long Island City, Queens and sells theatrical rigging hardware.
